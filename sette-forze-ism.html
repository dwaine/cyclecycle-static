Sette Forza ISM

Without much information The Editor purchased this frameset from an online auction service.

Before purchasing The Editor didn't find a lot of information about this model. After assembling into a complete bicycle he can make some comments:

  1. Full carbon fork with tapered (1 1/8 to 1 1/5 inch) steerer allows a pretty light and stiff fork
  2. Traditional 68mm bottom bracket is a surprise in this era of oversize bottom bracket standards. But that's OK because The Editor prefers outboard 24mm bearing systems.
  3. Internal rear brake cable routing utilizes a full length cable sheath AND has a guide tube inside the top tube. No need to snake a bare brake cable through an open top tube!
  4. Rear stays are curvy! (For better or worse)
  5. Plenty of tire clearance. 25mm Michelin Lithium 2 tires fit with room to spare.
  6. Top headset bearing is IS42. Bottom headset bearing is IS52.
  7. Seat mast cap allows only 10mm of adjustment. You better nail the measurements before cutting the seat mast to length.

The flagship of the Sette road line begins with the Forza. Using the latest in Hi Modulus carbon fiber technology, the Forza uses a 3k filament weave for efficiency in strength to weight ratios. The Forza takes advantage of an integrated seat mast, a tapered head tube/fork system and aero efficient tubing, every detail is covered to keep you on the front with the Forza.
